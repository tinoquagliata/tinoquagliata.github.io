That's my DJ

This is a byline.

Baltimore Sun

February 19, 2021

In his senior year of high school, DJ LeMahieu entered a milk chugging contest. His all-boys high school in Bloomfield Hills, Mich., had organized a tournament in which students from each grade would compete against one another in a series of challenges. They could sign up for a basketball tournament, a wiffle ball home run derby, a flag football match and other various events.

Milk

LeMahieu likely would have dominated any of the sports-related competitions he would have entered. He was a skilled baseball and basketball player, and his natural athleticism and determination seemed to allow him to excel at whichever competition he tried.

“He knew if he played a basketball game in front of the whole school or if he went out and played football, he would win all of it. There was a home run derby for wiffleball and everyone said, ‘Oh, DJ is gonna hit like 60 bombs,’ and he didn’t sign up for it,” said Brad Galli, a close friend of LeMahieu’s from Michigan. ”I’m pretty sure milk was the only thing that he wanted to do that day, and he devoted all his effort into doing it.”

So, when he was given his options his senior year — the sports he was already known to be good at, or a new challenge that could probably contribute more win probability for the senior boys — he chose the chugging.
